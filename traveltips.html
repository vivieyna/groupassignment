Below is some travel tips for you!

1. Safety and healthy

🕶 Keep Yourself Hydrated: The tropical weather can be steamy and muggy. To keep hydrated, sip lots of water.

🕶 Sun Protection: To shield oneself from the harsh sun, wear sunglasses, a hat, and sunscreen.

🕶 First Aid Kit: Keep a simple kit with bandages, antiseptic, and prescription drugs on hand.

🕶 Mosquito Repellent: Bring bug repellent to protect yourself from bites, particularly after dark and in areas close to the forest.

2. Money and budgeting

💵 Cash: Although the island has ATMs, not all establishments take credit cards. Carry enough cash to cover meals and modest purchases.

💵 Bargaining: In local marketplaces, haggling is typical. Never be frightened to negotiate for a better deal, but do it civilly.

3. Languange and communication

🔥 Basic Malay terms: Learn a few basic Malay terms. Visitors may be proficient in English, but the locals will appreciate the effort.

🔥 Network connection: A local SIM card might be a good idea if you need a constant internet connection, though many cafes and hotels offer free Wi-Fi.

4. Cultural courtesies

👕 Dress Modestly: When visiting temples or nearby villages, especially, dress modestly outside beach areas.

👕 Observe Local Traditions: Recognise the traditions and customs of the area. Take off your shoes, for example, when you enter a temple or someone's house.

5. Packing list essentials

🎐 Light Clothes: Bring breezy, light apparel appropriate for a tropical environment.

🎐 Beach Gear and Swimwear: Remember to pack flip-flops, a beach towel, and swimwear.

🎐 Comfy Footwear: Bring comfortable shoes to stroll or hike.

🎐 Reusable Bag: A reusable bag is practical for shopping or packing necessities for the beach.

6. Sustainability and the environment

💗 Respect Wildlife: Do not disturb animals and keep a safe distance.

💗 Avoid Littering: To help keep the island clean, dispose of waste appropriately, and think about cleaning up litter when you see it.

💗 Conserve Resources: Be wise with water and electricity to support the island's sustainability initiatives.

7. Emergencies

🌞 Be Familiar with Emergency Numbers: Keep your phone's emergency numbers saved. The general emergency number for Malaysia is 999.

🌞 Local Connections: Ensure you have the contact details for your lodging and any local connections.

8. Timing your visit

💌 Off-Peak Seasons: Try to visit during off-peak hours to avoid crowds and save money.

💌 Weather Conditions: Check the weather report and prepare for tropical downpours, especially if you're traveling during the monsoon season.
